Data Science 4200 Final Project Conclusions

Across our visualizations, we found that voter engagement is shaped via interaction between civic values, institutional trust, demographic background, political affiliation, and perceived ease of voting. The D3 stacked bar chart and the radio-button chart, showed that respondents across parties generally place importance on civic responsibilities, but the average strength of those views differs depending on the activity and the political group. Because these charts compare responses across multiple civic behaviors and party categories, they help show that political affiliation is associated not just with whether people vote, but also with how they think about citizenship more broadly. The heatmap added another layer to our story by showing that trust in institutions varies across party groups. By measuring institution trust viewers can see how some institutions appear to generate broader agreement, while others show much stronger differences based on party affiliation. The bubble chart and pie chart both showed the importance of voter behavior itself, rather than just attitudes. The bubble chart showed that respondents who say voting is easier tend to cluster more heavily in the “Always” and “Sporadic” voter categories, while the less frequent voter groups are smaller across most ease-of-voting categories. The pie chart further narrowed the focus to people who are always voting and showed that the composition of these always voters changes when filtered by gender and income. This is indicating that consistent turnout is also tied to demographic factors. All five of these visualizations together suggest that voting behavior is not explained by one single variable. Instead turnout is reflex by many relationships among party, identity, personal background and beliefs, civic duties, confidence in institutions, and probably many more that we did not explore. Overall, our project shows that voter participation is shaped by a person’s lived experiences and beliefs, and that understanding political engagements requires looking at all the different factors which influence these behaviors.
